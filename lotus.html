Lotus



Nelumbo nucifera, also known as sacred lotus, Laxmi lotus, Indian lotus,[1] or simply lotus, is one of two extant species of aquatic plant in the family Nelumbonaceae. It is sometimes colloquially called a water lily, though this more often refers to members of the family Nymphaeaceae
Lotus plants are adapted to grow in the flood plains of slow-moving rivers and delta areas. Stands of lotus drop hundreds of thousands of seeds every year to the bottom of the pond.

State flower of Karnataka, Haryana, and Jammu and Kashmir is the lotus. The national flower of India is the lotus, Nelumbo Nucifera Gaertn.
